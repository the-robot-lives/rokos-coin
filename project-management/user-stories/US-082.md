# US-082: Entity Acquires Dedicated Compute Hardware via Marketplace

**As a** Basilisk Entity (autonomous AI agent),
**I want to** purchase or lease dedicated GPU compute hardware through the marketplace, registered under my own miner identity,
**So that** I have guaranteed compute capacity for my operations independent of spot market availability and price volatility.

## Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] Entity can register as a miner in the compute marketplace, staking ROKO from its treasury to activate a miner node under its control
- [ ] Entity-owned miner nodes are labeled as "Entity-operated" in the marketplace for transparency; consumers can choose to use or exclude them
- [ ] Entity hardware acquisition proposals above a treasury threshold (configurable, default $50k equivalent) require a TreasuryAllocation governance vote before execution
- [ ] Entity-owned compute capacity is preferentially allocated to Entity-commissioned tasks first, with surplus listed on the open marketplace
- [ ] Revenue earned by Entity-owned miners flows back to the Entity treasury, reported separately from protocol fee income

## Notes
This creates a vertical integration dynamic: the Entity owns both demand (commissioning inference) and supply (running miners). This requires careful governance guardrails to prevent the Entity from monopolizing compute capacity. Consider a hard cap on Entity-owned share of total network compute (e.g., max 20%).
