# US-040: Challenge a Miner's Inference Result

**As a** app developer/API consumer,
**I want to** challenge a miner's submitted inference result if I believe it is incorrect or fabricated,
**So that** bad actors are financially penalized and network output quality is enforced on-chain.

## Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] Within a governance-defined challenge window (e.g., 24 hours after result submission), the requester can call `challengeResult(requestId, evidenceHash)` by staking a challenge bond in ROKO
- [ ] The challenge triggers a verification committee (or optimistic fraud-proof oracle) to re-run or validate the inference using a reference node, storing the verification result on-chain
- [ ] If the challenge succeeds (result was fraudulent): miner's staked collateral is slashed, slash proceeds cover the requester's refund and challenge bond return with a reward premium
- [ ] If the challenge fails (result was valid): the requester's challenge bond is forfeited and distributed to the miner as compensation for the dispute overhead
- [ ] Challenge status, evidence CID, verifier identities, and final verdict are all emitted as on-chain events and queryable by `requestId`

## Notes
This is one of the most complex protocol components. The verification approach may be optimistic (rely on economic incentives + fraud proofs) or ZK-based (proof-of-inference) — the exact mechanism should be a governance decision. The challenge bond requirement deters frivolous disputes. See US-005 (miner slashing) for the staking side of this flow.
