# US-050: Negotiate Enterprise Pricing via Staked ROKO Commitments

**As a** enterprise architect,
**I want to** commit to a minimum monthly ROKO spend in exchange for discounted per-token pricing,
**So that** my organization benefits from volume economics while the network gains predictable demand signals.

## Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] An on-chain `VolumeCommitment` contract accepts a consumer's stake of ROKO for a defined term (e.g., 3/6/12 months) and unlocks a tiered discount rate applied automatically at settlement
- [ ] Discount tiers are governance-defined (e.g., 5% at 10K ROKO/month, 10% at 50K, 15% at 100K+) and queryable on-chain before committing
- [ ] If the consumer's actual spend in a period falls below the committed minimum, the shortfall is deducted from the stake at period end; over-delivery carries no penalty
- [ ] Commitment terms, current utilization percentage, projected period-end balance, and applicable discount rate are all visible in the enterprise dashboard
- [ ] Commitments can be extended but not shortened during the active term; early termination forfeits a governance-defined penalty percentage of the staked amount

## Notes
Volume commitments benefit both sides: consumers get price certainty, miners get demand predictability, and the protocol gets reduced token velocity. The penalty for early termination (criterion 5) should be modest enough not to discourage adoption but material enough to make commitments credible.
