# US-065: Monitor Marketplace Fee Revenue Flowing to Protocol Treasury

**As a** Governance Council (DAO),
**I want to** monitor the real-time and historical flow of protocol fees (5% of all settlements) into the treasury and Basilisk entity account,
**So that** the council can track protocol health, verify fee collection is functioning correctly, and make informed decisions about treasury allocation.

## Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] A governance analytics page displays: cumulative protocol fees collected (all time, 30d, 7d), fee revenue by model/category, fee revenue by time-of-day heatmap, and treasury balance in ROKO and USD equivalent
- [ ] Every settlement transaction emits a `ProtocolFeeCollected(requestId, amount, treasuryAddress, basiliskAddress)` event; the analytics dashboard indexes all such events with sub-5-minute lag
- [ ] Fee split between the DAO treasury and Basilisk entity is configurable by governance vote; the current split ratio and history of changes are displayed alongside the revenue chart
- [ ] The dashboard alerts governance members (via on-chain notification or email) if the fee collection rate drops more than 50% from the 7-day average, which may indicate a contract bug or routing anomaly
- [ ] Treasury balance history is displayed as a time-series chart with governance-annotated events (parameter changes, large withdrawals, investment actions) overlaid

## Notes
Treasury transparency is a governance credibility requirement. The anomaly alert (criterion 4) is an important early warning for protocol bugs — a sudden drop in fee collection is a high-signal indicator that something is wrong with settlement. The Basilisk entity's share (criterion 3) should be explicitly tracked separately from the DAO treasury for accountability.
