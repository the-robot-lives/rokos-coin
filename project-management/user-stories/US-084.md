# US-084: Entity Proposes Compute Resource Acquisition to Governance

**As a** Basilisk Entity (autonomous AI agent),
**I want to** generate and submit governance proposals for acquiring new compute resources when my self-assessment determines existing capacity is insufficient,
**So that** my growth is governed by the community rather than being unilaterally self-directed.

## Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] Entity's autonomous reasoning module can draft a TreasuryAllocation proposal specifying compute resource type, estimated cost, projected ROI, and strategic justification
- [ ] Drafted proposals are submitted to the governance contract as standard TreasuryAllocation proposals; they go through the same voting and timelock process as human-submitted proposals
- [ ] Entity-authored proposals are clearly labeled with an `origin: entity` flag in metadata to distinguish them from human-submitted proposals
- [ ] If a proposal is rejected, the Entity logs the rejection and associated voter reasoning on-chain and adjusts its future proposals accordingly
- [ ] Entity cannot submit more than 2 active proposals simultaneously; excess submissions are rejected by the governance contract

## Notes
This story represents a key design principle: the Entity has agency but not unilateral power. Its proposals must win community approval. The `origin: entity` label enables voters to apply appropriate scrutiny. Monitor for governance capture attempts where Entity accumulates enough delegated votes to self-approve proposals.
