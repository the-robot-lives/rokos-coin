# Sofia Reyes

## Demographics
- **Role:** Independent crypto investor and DeFi power user; part-time governance participant across 6+ protocols
- **Technical Level:** High in DeFi mechanics, tokenomics, and on-chain analytics; moderate in AI/ML; low in GPU infrastructure specifics
- **Context:** Has been in crypto since 2017. Holds a diversified portfolio across L1s, DeFi yield positions, and infrastructure tokens. Allocated 4% of her portfolio to ROKO after the TGE based on the proof-of-inference narrative and the basilisk entity concept. She actively manages her position — staking, yield farming, and governance voting.

## Goals
1. Generate yield on ROKO holdings through staking, liquidity provision, or delegated compute
2. Participate meaningfully in governance to protect the long-term value of her position
3. Maintain a real-time view of tokenomics health: emission rate, circulating supply, treasury balance, staking ratio
4. Exit or rebalance the position efficiently when needed; requires deep liquidity on major DEXs
5. Understand the basilisk entity's treasury as a fundamental driver of token value accrual

## Frustrations
1. Emission schedule is not clearly documented; hard to model future dilution
2. Staking rewards compete with liquidity provision rewards — not enough tooling to optimize across both
3. Governance proposals are technical and dense; no plain-language summaries for non-engineer voters
4. Treasury transparency is insufficient — cannot verify basilisk entity holdings or spending on-chain without significant manual effort
5. No portfolio dashboard that aggregates ROKO positions, staking rewards, and governance activity in one view
6. Slashing mechanics are opaque — unclear what validator behavior triggers slashing and at what magnitude

## Relationship to ROKO Protocol

She is also a key signal amplifier whose public analysis shapes retail sentiment.
