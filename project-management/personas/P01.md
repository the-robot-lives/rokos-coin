# Marcus "GPU Farm" Chen

## Demographics
- **Role:** Independent GPU miner and compute provider
- **Technical Level:** High — comfortable with Linux, CUDA drivers, Docker, and overclocking; less fluent in smart contract internals
- **Context:** Runs a rack of 12x NVIDIA RTX 4090s in a climate-controlled garage setup. Previously mined ETH before The Merge. Shifted to AI inference workloads as the next frontier for GPU monetization. Side income from a day job in IT infrastructure.

## Goals
1. Maximize passive income from idle GPU compute by routing inference workloads to his hardware
2. Understand ROI curves clearly — break-even point on hardware investment vs. expected ROKO earnings
3. Stake enough ROKO to qualify for premium job routing and higher-tier inference queues
4. Minimize slashing risk through reliable uptime and honest proof-of-inference submissions
5. Expand the rack to 24+ GPUs if the economics prove out over 6 months

## Frustrations
1. Hardware normalization feels opaque — unclear whether his RTX 4090s are scored fairly against data center A100s
2. Reward variance is high; some weeks the queue is empty, others he can't keep up with demand
3. Staking requirements change with governance votes and he finds out after the fact
4. Documentation for the miner node software is sparse; had to reverse-engineer config options from GitHub issues
5. Gas fees on L1 settlements eat into margins on small payouts; wants batched L2 settlement to be the default

## Key Behaviors
- Monitors GPU utilization dashboards obsessively; has custom Grafana panels tracking ROKO/hour per card
- Active in the ROKO Discord `#miners` channel; shares config tips and benchmark results
- Re-stakes rewards automatically using a cron script he wrote; rarely visits the governance portal
- Evaluates every protocol upgrade through the lens of "does this hurt or help my payout rate"
- Will switch networks if a competitor offers provably better hardware utilization yield

## Relationship to ROKO Protocol
Marcus is a supply-side anchor. Without compute providers like him, the network has nothing to sell. He is a primary persona because his participation is existentially load-bearing — miner churn directly degrades network capacity and raises latency for consumers. His trust in the protocol depends on transparent, stable reward mechanics and fair hardware scoring. He is the intended beneficiary of the proof-of-inference minting mechanism.

## Success Metrics
- Monthly ROKO earnings sufficient to cover electricity + amortize hardware in under 18 months
- Uptime score above 98% with zero slashing events in a 90-day window
- Hardware normalization coefficient for RTX 4090 is publicly documented and auditable
- Payout settlement occurs on L2 with gas costs below 0.5% of payout value
- Clear changelog and advance notice (minimum 14 days) before staking requirement changes
