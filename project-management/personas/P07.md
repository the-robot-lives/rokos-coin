# Protocol Governance Council

## Demographics
- **Role:** Composite persona representing the active DAO membership responsible for on-chain governance of the ROKO protocol
- **Technical Level:** Mixed — ranges from highly technical core contributors to economically-sophisticated token holders with limited contract knowledge; the council's effective competence is the intersection of these skill sets
- **Context:** Comprises approximately 40-200 active governance participants (depending on proposal significance) drawn from miners, investors, developers, and researchers. Collectively holds the delegated authority to modify protocol parameters, allocate treasury funds, upgrade contracts, and set slashing thresholds. Operates via on-chain proposal/vote cycles with an off-chain discussion phase on the governance forum.

## Goals
1. Maintain emission rates that balance miner incentives against long-term token supply sustainability
2. Set slashing thresholds that deter fraud without punishing honest operational failures (hardware crashes, network outages)
3. Allocate treasury funds to maximize protocol capability expansion: audits, grants, infrastructure, partnerships
4. Ensure fee split between miners, the basilisk entity treasury, and the protocol reserve remains economically viable for all parties
5. Grow active governance participation beyond the current core contributor concentration

## Frustrations
1. Voter apathy — quorum is frequently missed on important proposals, concentrating power in a small active cohort
2. Proposal complexity creates information asymmetry; technically dense proposals favor insiders who understand the code
3. Treasury allocation decisions are contentious — no formal framework for evaluating competing funding requests
4. Emergency governance actions (protocol pause, parameter freeze) require speed incompatible with standard vote cycles
5. Basilisk entity treasury growth and spending is under-scrutinized because most participants don't understand its mechanics
6. Governance attacks via flash-loan vote manipulation are a latent risk not yet adequately mitigated

## Key Behaviors
- Hosts bi-weekly governance calls open to all token holders; publishes summaries in the forum
- Maintains a proposal tracker with status, voting deadlines, and implementation timelines
- Routes all contract upgrades through the security auditor (P05) before final vote
- Delegates technical review of parameter change proposals to a standing Technical Advisory Committee
- Produces quarterly treasury reports covering inflows, outflows, and basilisk entity asset holdings

## Relationship to ROKO Protocol
The Governance Council is the protocol's immune system and steering mechanism simultaneously. Their decisions set the economic conditions under which every other persona operates — miner reward rates, consumer fee levels, slashing risk, and the basilisk entity's resource budget. A well-functioning council enables the protocol to adapt to market conditions and security discoveries. A dysfunctional or captured council can drain the treasury, demoralize miners, and break consumer trust through erratic parameter changes. Their success is measured not by any single vote but by the long-term health of all protocol stakeholders.

## Success Metrics
- Quorum achieved on at least 90% of governance proposals without requiring emergency extension
- Proposal plain-language summaries published for 100% of proposals within 24 hours of submission
- No treasury allocation to any single non-security initiative exceeds 10% without a supermajority vote
- Emergency governance module activated and tested — allows parameter freeze within 1 hour via multisig, subject to retroactive ratification within 72 hours
- Governance participation Gini coefficient below 0.6 (measuring delegate concentration)
- All contract upgrades require minimum 7-day timelock after governance approval
- Basilisk entity spending above a defined threshold requires explicit governance authorization
