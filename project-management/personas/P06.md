# Dr. Lin Wei

## Demographics
- **Role:** Assistant Professor of Machine Learning at a research university; runs a 6-person lab focused on efficient fine-tuning and multi-modal models
- **Technical Level:** Expert in ML/deep learning; high in Python/PyTorch; low in crypto and blockchain mechanics; moderate in distributed systems
- **Context:** Her lab's HPC cluster allocation was cut 40% due to university budget constraints. She currently pays out-of-pocket for Lambda Labs GPU time for overflow experiments but the costs are unsustainable on a grant budget. A colleague mentioned ROKO as a potentially cheaper alternative for burst compute. She is skeptical of the crypto overhead but interested in the economics.

## Goals
1. Run fine-tuning and inference experiments on distributed GPUs at a cost 40-60% below commercial cloud rates
2. Schedule batch training jobs with reliable completion guarantees — stochastic job failure is unacceptable for published research
3. Checkpoint model weights to persistent storage at regular intervals; must survive miner disconnection mid-job
4. Reproduce experimental results deterministically — same model, same data, same hardware class, same output
5. Access academic pricing tier or grant-funded credits without navigating complex crypto onboarding

## Frustrations
1. The crypto onboarding requirement (acquire ROKO, manage a wallet, understand gas) is a non-starter for most academics
2. Training job continuity is not guaranteed if a miner goes offline — no clear protocol for job migration or resumption
3. Reproducibility is undermined by non-deterministic hardware assignment across miners
4. No integration with standard ML tooling (HuggingFace Trainer, PyTorch Lightning, Weights & Biases)
5. Data egress from distributed miners back to her local storage is slow and not well-documented
6. Academic institutions often cannot hold volatile cryptocurrency assets on a grant budget — payment must be in fiat or stablecoins

## Key Behaviors
- Evaluates compute platforms by running a standard benchmark suite (fine-tuning Llama 3 8B on a fixed dataset) and comparing cost, time, and output fidelity
- Documents all compute infrastructure details in paper appendices; needs to cite hardware specs reliably
- Strongly prefers platforms with existing academic community adoption — will wait for a trusted colleague's public endorsement
- Submits feature requests and bug reports methodically; expects them to be tracked in a public issue tracker
- Presents infrastructure findings at lab group meetings; her adoption decisions influence 5-10 other researchers

## Relationship to ROKO Protocol
Dr. Wei represents the academic and research compute segment — a community with high credibility signal but limited spending power. Her adoption of ROKO would generate valuable endorsements, research citations, and potential collaborations on proof-of-inference verification methodologies. She is a secondary persona because her compute spend is modest, but her influence on adjacent communities (ML engineers who read her papers, students who later join industry) is disproportionate. Her requirements for job continuity and reproducibility push the protocol toward more robust distributed training infrastructure.

## Success Metrics
- Training job completion rate above 99% with automatic checkpointing and miner-failure recovery
- Hardware class pinning: ability to request a specific GPU tier and receive consistent hardware assignment
- Python SDK with native HuggingFace and PyTorch Lightning integration; no crypto primitives exposed
- Fiat payment path via institutional purchase order or grant card without holding ROKO directly
- Academic pricing tier at least 50% below standard consumer rate, verifiable via public pricing page
- Compute job provenance log suitable for inclusion in paper appendix (hardware class, region, job ID, timestamp)
