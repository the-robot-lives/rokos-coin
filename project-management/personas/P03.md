# David Nakamura

## Demographics
- **Role:** Principal Enterprise Architect at a Fortune 500 financial services firm; leads AI infrastructure strategy
- **Technical Level:** Very high in distributed systems and cloud architecture; moderate in blockchain (understands concepts, skeptical of operational maturity); high in compliance and risk frameworks
- **Context:** His firm runs internal LLM-based document processing workflows that currently cost $2.1M/year on Azure OpenAI. His mandate is to evaluate alternative compute strategies that reduce vendor lock-in and cost. ROKO crossed his desk via a vendor briefing. He is conducting formal due diligence over a 60-day evaluation window.

## Goals
1. Reduce AI inference costs at scale (millions of API calls/day) without sacrificing reliability
2. Eliminate single-vendor dependency — distribute inference across multiple providers with guaranteed SLA failover
3. Ensure data privacy: input documents must not be logged, retained, or accessible to third-party hardware operators
4. Meet SOC 2 Type II and FINRA compliance requirements for any infrastructure that touches sensitive financial data
5. Establish contractual SLAs with defined uptime guarantees and financial remedies for breach

## Frustrations
1. Decentralized networks have no legal entity to sign an enterprise contract or SLA with
2. Data residency requirements conflict with the nature of distributed compute — he cannot guarantee which jurisdiction processes a request
3. No enterprise support tier; GitHub issues and Discord are not acceptable escalation paths for production incidents
4. Token volatility introduces unpredictable cost variance into budget planning cycles
5. Audit trails for inference calls are insufficient for compliance documentation needs
6. TEE attestation is promising but the attestation verification tooling is not mature enough for his risk team

## Key Behaviors
- Produces formal evaluation reports with risk matrices and scored vendor comparison rubrics
- Engages legal and compliance teams before any technical pilot; will not run production traffic without both sign-offs
- Tests disaster recovery scenarios explicitly — simulates miner failures, network partitions, and oracle outages
- Requests architectural briefings from the core team; evaluates team quality as a proxy for protocol longevity
- Participates in governance forums if the protocol offers an enterprise advisory channel

## Relationship to ROKO Protocol
David represents the high-value enterprise segment that could dramatically increase protocol revenue but requires the protocol to mature beyond its current Web3-native assumptions. His adoption is a long-horizon outcome contingent on TEE attestation maturity, legal entity formation, and enterprise-grade tooling. He is a secondary persona today but a primary revenue driver in year 2-3. His concerns about compliance and data privacy directly shape the protocol's roadmap for confidential compute and audit logging features.

## Success Metrics
- TEE attestation covers 100% of inference calls for sensitive workloads, with verifiable audit logs
- Data residency controls allow geographic routing constraints (e.g., EU-only miners for GDPR compliance)
- Enterprise support tier with named account manager and SLA-backed incident response
- Cost at scale ($500K+/year commitment) is at least 25% lower than Azure OpenAI equivalent
- Legal entity or foundation able to execute enterprise master service agreements
- Formal third-party security audit report available for protocol and TEE implementation
